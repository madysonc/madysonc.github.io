  • Mercury

    Diameter of planet:3,031 miles
    Surface conditions: covered in craters and very rocky
    Rotation period: It takes Mercury about 59 Earth days to spin once on its axis (the rotation period), and about 88 Earth days to complete one orbit about the Sun. However, the length of the day on Mercury is 176 Earth days.
    Mass of the planet compared to Earth: The mass of Mercury is 3.3 x 1023 kg. It sounds like a big number, but it's really only 5.5% the mass of Earth.
    Natural satellites of the planet: 0
    Number of rings: 0
    Interesting fact: Your weight on Mercury would be 38% of your weight on Earth.

  • Earth

    Period of revolution: 365 days
    Diameter of planet: 12 742 km
    Surface conditions: Most of the Earth's surface (70%) is covered with water, and the remaining 30% is taken up by the seven continental landmasses. However, underneath the water that fills the oceans, and the dirt and plants that cover the continents, the Earth's surface layer is made of rock.
    Rotation period: The Earth rotates once in about 24 hours with respect to the sun and once every 23 hours, 56 minutes and 4 seconds.
    Mass of the planet compared to Earth: umm… it is earth
    Natural satellites of each planet: 1
    Number of rings: 0
    Interesting fact: Earth’s rotation is gradually slowing. This deceleration is happening almost imperceptibly, at approximately 17 milliseconds per hundred years, although the rate at which it occurs is not perfectly uniform. This has the effect of lengthening our days, but it happens so slowly that it could be as much as 140 million years before the length of a day will have increased to 25 hours.

  • Jupiter

    Period of revolution: 12 years
    Diameter of planet: 139 822 km
    Surface conditions: Jupiter is called a gas giant planet. Its atmosphere is made up of mostly hydrogen gas and helium gas, like the sun. The planet is covered in thick red, brown, yellow and white clouds. The clouds make the planet look like it has stripes.
    Rotation period: 0d 9h 56m
    Mass of the planet compared to Earth: Earth's has a mean radius of 6,371 km (3,958.8 mi), and a mass of 5.97 × 1024 kg, whereas Jupiter has a mean radius of 69,911 ± 6 km (43441 mi) and a mass of 1.8986×1027 kg. In short, Jupiter is almost 11 times the size of Earth, and just under 318 times as massive.
    Natural satellites of each planet: 67
    Number of rings: 4
    Interesting fact: Jupiter has the shortest day of all the planets. It turns on its axis once every 9 hours and 55 minutes. The rapid rotation flattens the planet slightly, giving it an oblate shape.

  • Saturn

    Period of revolution: 29 years
    Diameter of planet: 116 464 km
    Surface conditions: Saturn is a ball made up almost entirely of hydrogen and helium. The density and temperature changes the deeper into the planet you go, but Saturn can't be said to have a solid surface. ... And below that, where temperatures are 0-degrees C, there are clouds of water.
    Rotation period: 0d 10h 42m
    Mass of the planet compared to Earth: the mass of Saturn is 95 times the mass of the Earth
    Natural satellites of each planet: 67 and 4 major moons
    Number of rings: Saturn has four main groups of rings and three fainter, narrower ring groups. These groups are separated by gaps called divisions. Close up views of Saturn's rings by the Voyager spacecrafts, which flew by them in 1980 and 1981, showed that these seven ring groups are made up of thousands of smaller rings. The exact number is not known.
    Interesting fact: Saturn orbits the Sun once every 29.4 Earth years.
    Its slow movement against the backdrop of stars earned it the nickname of “Lubadsagush” from the ancient Assyrians. The name means “oldest of the old”.

  • Pluto

    Period of revolution: 247.92 years
    Diameter of planet: 2372 km
    Surface conditions: Pluto is thought to be made of mostly ice. It probably also has a small rocky core which might contain some metals. The ice on the surface of Pluto is made of frozen nitrogen, methane and carbon monoxide.
    Rotation period: 6.39
    Mass of the planet compared to Earth: The mass of Pluto is 1.3 x 1022 kg, which is only 0.2% the mass of the Earth, or 18% the mass of the Moon. Needless to say, Pluto doesn't have very much mass at all.
    Natural satellites of the planet: 5
    Number of rings: Does Pluto have rings? Astronomers have no idea. Pluto is so far away that it’s impossible to get a clear view of Pluto from here on Earth. But scientists think that it’s possible that Pluto does have rings. This idea comes from the fact that Pluto has two tiny moons, Nix and Hydra. They’re just a few km across, and have very little gravity. So any micrometeoroid impacts on these moons will kick up material into orbit around Pluto. Instead of falling back down onto the moons, this impact material would drift into rings around Pluto. Astronomers think it could actually survive for up to 100,000 years. This is a similar process that creates some of the rings around Saturn and Jupiter. If this is true, it would constitute the first set of rings around a solid object (in this case a dwarf planet), rather than a gas giant planet. When NASA’s New Horizons mission arrives at Pluto in 2015, it might be able to detect these faint rings, and confirm the theory.
    Interesting fact: Pluto sometimes has an atmosphere. When Pluto elliptical orbit takes it closer to the Sun, its surface ice thaws and forms a thin atmosphere primarily of nitrogen which slowly escapes the planet. It also has a methane haze that overs about 161 kilometres above the surface. The methane is dissociated by sunlight into hydrocarbons that fall to the surface and coat the ice with a dark covering. When Pluto travels away from the Sun the atmosphere then freezes back to its solid state.
